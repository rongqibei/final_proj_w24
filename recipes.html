üçõ Curry Pork Chop with Rice

Ingredients:

  • Boneless pork chops
  • S&B Golden Curry Sauce Mix packet
  • OnionsüßÖ, carrotsü•ï, potatoesü•î
  • Panko breadcrumbs
  • Eggs

Instructions

  1. Peel and chop the onion, carrots, and potatoes into bite-sized pieces.
  2. In a large pot, add the vegetables and 3 cups of water. Bring to a boil and then reduce the heat to simmer until the vegetables are soft.
  3. Stir in the S&B Golden Curry Sauce Mix and simmer until the sauce thickens. If the sauce is too thick, add water to reach your desired consistency.
  4. While the curry is simmering, prepare the pork chops. Season them with salt and pepper. Dredge each pork chop in flour, dip into the beaten egg, and coat with panko breadcrumbs.
  5. Heat vegetable oil in a pan and fry the pork chops until golden brown and cooked through. Let them rest for a few minutes, then slice into strips.
  6. Serve the curry over a bed of fluffy rice, place the crispy pork chop on the side, and sprinkle with black sesame seeds for garnish.
  7. Enjoy your "homemade" curry pork chop with rice!

ü¶ÄÔ∏è Spicy Fried Crab with Rice Cake

Ingredients:

  • Crab legs (from Costco)
  • Rice cake
  • Dry red pepper üå∂Ô∏è and spring onion
  • Flour and eggs
  • Pixian bean paste

Instructions

  1. Coat the crab legs in egg liquid and flour, then fry them in a pan until golden brown to enrich the texture.
  2. Boil the rice cake.
  3. Heat oil in a new pot. Add minced garlic, dried chili, Sichuan peppercorns, and ginger to the pot, and fry until aromatic. Then, re-add the fried crab legs. Continuously stir-fry with Pixian bean paste and oyster sauce.
  4. Pour in some beer and water to the pot, add the rice cake, and simmer on low heat for a while (be careful not to add too much water as it will take longer to cook).
  5. Turn up the heat, making sure the crab legs are coated with the sauce. Be careful not to let the rice cake stick to the pan during this step.
  6. Serve the dish on a plate, and it's ready to enjoy!

  • Pixian bean paste

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan and cook the ground pork until it just starts to change color. Then add Pixian bean paste to flavor.
  2. Add minced ginger, minced garlic, and chopped tomatoes, and stir-fry until the tomatoes release their juice. Season with two spoons of light soy sauce and one spoon of oyster sauce.
  3. Add boiling water to the mixture.
  4. Put in the pre-cooked rice noodles and let them soak up the flavors.
  5. Serve and enjoy your meal!
